Customer Review Analysis In Accordance With Product Analysis

Literature Review

Introduction

In recent years, we have seen immense growth in e-commerce, the products which were sold in retail businesses in the past decade have started appearing in the inventories of big online e-commerce platforms. Hence data around products and their review in raw form of text has enlarged and the technology which analyzes this large amount of text is also evolving. For example, Customer reviews which once were not available to the hospitality operators has been a deciding factor in formulation of business processes (Lui et. al, 2015). A segment of research is leaning toward how users perceive the review content that is available on the internet. As an example, a recent survey taken in 2021 suggests that 54% of the user review content is trusted by the respondents in the US and with a high number in Great Britain (2). However, the amount of data that is available on the internet and e-commerce websites is a huge amount of unstructured data either in terms of customer reviews or social media content and it is the most challenging task in big data analytics to extract information from unstructured text (Gandomi & Haider, 2015).

To understand the customer satisfaction and to further extend that to improve business process and product design process, customer review analysis can be exploited as a vital factor. Previously, Kano's model (Kano, 1984) provided a framework comprising five quality attributes to explain the process of customer satisfaction. This has been exploited in various studies to extract features from textual reviews and have been improved upon to better understand the customer expectation from the product and hence the product features.

In this paper, we will analyze available literature on customer review analysis in respect with product analyses. Throughout this paper we will touch upon major ideas as well as approaches used to mine customer textual reviews and social media content with various semantic analysis techniques as well as other machine learning algorithms.

Major Themes

The literature review revealed major themes regarding meaning in the textual reviews and their interpretation by the reader. Various studies used historical knowledge on sentiment analysis and understanding of customer psychology.

The literature reviewed in this study showed major themes related to :

  1. Kano model for customer satisfaction
  2. Polarity in customer reviews
  3. Helpfulness of Review
  4. Review Quality Classification
  5. Domain Knowledge
  6. Rating Framework
  7. Product Lifecycle
  8. Preference of Risk

Kano's model for customer satisfaction

The studies on product analysis and customer review analysis used semantic analysis to mine the unstructured text, however most of these studies were dependent on Kano's model for customer satisfaction (Kano, 1984) and its five categories of quality attribute. One such study weighed on five steps for extracting the customer satisfaction for hotels in Korea, which used frequency analysis and semantic analysis and then used Kano classification to derive the attributes that can represent Customer Satisfaction Model(Lee et. al, 2022). Another such study which applied Kano's model for customer satisfaction on bicycles found that the lifecycle of the product is a potent factor to consider when identifying the attributes from Kano's model and weighed on further study(Lin et. al, 2017). Another study concluded that deriving Kano's model categories from the importance of product or service was more effective to understand customer satisfaction for Huawei P series phones.(Wu et. al, 2018)

Polarity in customer reviews

Customer reviews for a product or service contains positive and negative sentiments, and this polarity in customer opinions for a single product is a major factor to consider when applying semantic analysis on customer reviews. This polarity in customer reviews was most effectively presented in study of discriminative attributes in the reviews of cosmetic products (Kim & Kang, 2018). Most of the studies which mined the text to arrive at what customer wants from a product included polarity based attribute classification. In one such study, the authors explored electronic word of mouth(eWOM) for e-commerce products using automatic fuzzy product ontology weighed on computation of polarity for more accurate results of eWOM(Sun et. al( 2019), Turney & Litmman (2003)) describe the classification of text into praise and criticism using semantic orientation and semantic association. Similarly, in another study authors explain how the polarity in customer reviews can be viewed in perspective of its helpfulness. (Ahmad & Laroche, 2015)

Helpfulness of review

Analysis of customer reviews only presents one part of the puzzle as it looks at the customer reviews subjectively and fails to provide understanding of the impact of these reviews on readers, hence fails to provide forecasting of the product sales(Lohan et. al, 2011). There have been studies to understand the impact of the reviews on the readers. Ahmad and Laroche (2015) proposed a hypothesis based on Cognitive Appraisal Theory suggesting that helpfulness of review is based on the certainty sentiment in the customer review. They concluded that the more the certainty sentiment in the customer review, the more is to direct the reader towards acceptance of the product. In another study, analyzing the impact of electronic word of mouth(eWOM) from social media platforms suggest that eWOM has a major role in reduction of uncertainty about the product and hence affects purchase decisions of customers(Hajli et. al, 2014)

Review of quality classification

Similar to helpfulness of the reviews, another approach that could be used to classify the reviews based on the quality of the reviews. By measuring the quality of the review, it provides a framework to remove them based on their quality. Such a similar approach can be seen in the study, the reviews are classified based on the opinion strength visualization and ranked based on their quality and removing low quality customer reviews(Shamin et. al, 2021). They used product features to develop a ranking method to evaluate customer reviews.

Domain Knowledge

Most of the time customer reviews are vague and contain no information about the product, if such reviews are credible or not is still a question. However, helpfulness of the review can be determined from domain knowledge in the customer review itself. In one such study where the authors try to improve SA(semantic analysis) technique using Domain Sensitive Features(DSF), emphasizes on use of domain knowledge to evaluate the bag of words(Rintyarna et.al, 2019). Similarly, authors have used labeled data to supervise the exploration of electronic word of mouth(eWOM) in customer reviews(Sun et. al, 2019). Domain knowledge is a very important aspect in such studies where social content is evaluated for its interpretation as it improves certainty in readers' minds approving the certainty hypothesis proposed by Ahmed and Laroche(Ahmad & Laroche, 2015).

Rating Framework

Today many online reviewing systems provide a differential rating that tells users to rate the product or service based on each attribute. These multidimensional rating frameworks can be useful to provide more informative ratings(Chen et. al, 2018). Peng and Cui provides an approach to understand the customer satisfaction for hotels(service) and beer(product) through correlation between different ratings and sentiments in textual reviews(Peng et. al, 2019). In one such study, where the main focus was product rating, authors reviewed various components other than textual review and was more product related affecting the rating of the product(Sánchez-Pérez et. al, 2021)

Product Lifecycle

Customer reviews on products can vary over time throughout the lifecycle of the product. It can be an important aspect to understand the differentiation of expectation between service and products(Ahmad & Laroche, 2015). A time series analysis in combination with the I-Kano matrix algorithm can assist analyzing change in customer review attribute over a period of time(Lee et. al, 2022). Lin and Tsai also suggest more research towards the impact of product lifecycle on customer reviews and ratings(Lin et. al, 2017)

Preference of risk

Kano's model on customer satisfaction suggests that there are three basic principles of loss and gain(Lin et. al, 2017) which are very critical to understand the customer's preference for risk taking.These principles explain the customer's attitude towards loss and gain regarding the product features, which can be used to interpret the customer review in respect to the reader and forecast the impact on product sale.

There are other approaches to study the product analysis that have been touched upon in this literature review, however they are important in respect of product launch as well as the marketing associated with it. In study analyzing twitter data in respect to the launch of Apple and Samsung products, proposes a social network analysis focusing on the conversation analysis to understand the electronic word of mouth(eWOM)(Lipizzi et. al, 2015). As noted in one study (Albayrak & Caber, 2013) product performance and customer satisfaction has an asymmetric and non-linear relationship and can be a potential aspect while evaluating performance of the product based on online customer reviews(OCR). Reviews are classified based on the structure of the content in three different categories: short, long and unstructured (Haddara et. al, 2020).

Analysis Approaches Used

Product analysis based on customer review analysis can be divided into two different steps: mining the unstructured text to either produce a bag of words or extract sentiments of the customer reviews. And then use the output of the first step to evaluate the review using different analytics and machine learning techniques to produce results that can be understood.

Most of the studies done in the past on customer reviews analysis have used Latent Semantic Analysis(LSA) and Latent Dirichlet Allocation(LDA). For example, Kim and Kang used LSA and LDA to extract discriminative attributes from cosmetic products(Kim & Kang, 2018). Similarly, in a study of the impact of social media on electronic word of mouth(eWOM), the authors used Singular Value Decomposition(SVD) algorithm in addition to LSA to extract SVD factors from the twitter conversations(Ahmad & Laroche, 2015). While in study by Xu and Wang uses Linear Regression Model to work upon features computed from the textual reviews by LSA(Xu et. al, 2017).

In some studies of customer review analysis, authors have used more fine grained steps that ranged from three to five to come to the end result. Lee, Park and Kwak proposed five steps which included two steps of Kano classification and I-Kano matrix analysis to extract kano attributes from textual reviews(Lee et. al, 2022). Ban & Kim (2019) analyzed airlines customer reviews data by introducing Machine Learning framework to evaluate the features extracted from Frequency analysis and Semantic Network Analysis. In one study of Amazon product reviews, authors used supervised Machine Learning algorithm with labeled domain knowledge after semantic analysis to ingest credibility aspects of customer reviews into the text mining(Rintyarna et.al, 2019).

Findings

Finding meaning in the unstructured textual reviews is a challenging task and various aspects like Kano Model (Lee et. al , 2022, Wu et. al, 2018) polarity in customer reviews (Ahmad & Laroche, 2015), Kano model (Hajli et. al , 2014) Quality of reviews (Shamin et. al, 2021) has been used to provide a direction for understanding the meaning in textual reviews. Some exploration in social conversations(Hajli et. al, 2014) and social media content(Sun et. al, 2019) proposed models to understand the impact of social media trends and conversation to determine the sales of the product after its initial launch.

However, semantic analysis is a major technique that is used in customer review analysis as well as various other important aspects affecting the product analysis, various other techniques (as shown in Rintyarna et.al 2019)are placed on top to evaluate the process behind customer review analysis and product feature analysis.

The previous work in the analysis of customer reviews and other aspects of the product analysis reported few problems regarding chronological nature of customer reviews and its impact to the product lifecycle as major problems to incorporate with semantic analysis.(Lin et. al, 2017) Shimi Naurin Ahmad and Michel Laroche advocates future research on comparison of service and product reviews and how intangible aspects of service affects the content of the reviews (Ahmad & Laroche, 2015)

Gaps in Literature

Throughout this section, we will discuss the literature gap and possible direction of further study to understand the product or service in accordance with customer satisfaction. We will also touch upon various aspects of customer satisfaction which are more related to organizations business processes regarding the development of certain products. Below are the research questions that's needed to study to understand the relationship between customer reviews, customer satisfaction and product/service sale.

  1. Does the intangible nature of service affect the content of the customer reviews resulting in positive and negative sentiments?
  2. How the lifecycle of the product can affect the customer reviews and satisfaction based on it's timespan or launch of new product?
  3. Can a digital campaign on social media affect the customer reviews positively or negatively?
  4. Can a service which is supportive to a product increase customer satisfaction beyond its correlation to product performance?

Difference between reviews of product and service

As suggested in a study by Ahmed and Laroche, there needs to be further study in the area of difference between reviews of product and service(Ahmad & Laroche, 2015). There are possibilities of difference in customer satisfaction towards a product and service due to the intangible nature of service. Furthermore, customer reviews on service needs to be supported by the evidence of transcripts between customers and service support systems. Similar to the impact of social electronic word of mouth on reduction of certainty(Hajli et. al, 2014), the impact of transcripts can be evaluated to better understand the customer satisfaction of service. Additionally, customer opinions for services tend to be more detailed when classified as negative, however it needs to be verified to understand the difference of opinion mining between product and service.

Customer satisfaction through the life cycle of the product

Product life cycle can vary from days to years, for example a food product is consumed on daily or weekly basis while an automobile product can survive up to decade. To understand the customer satisfaction this very aspect of the product needs to be incorporated in the framework of customer review analysis. We can see such evidence(Lee et. al, 2022), where I-Kano Matric can be used to incorporate additional variables like longevity of the product. Products which are having shorter life cycles can incorporate direct performance-importance(Wu et. al, 2018) models to improve customer satisfaction. Similar kind of study needs to be done having a longer life cycle, to improve the product design processes that can take continuous evaluation from customer review mining.

Correlation between digital marketing and its reflection in customer reviews.

There is indeed an impact of social media content on the interpretation of customer reviews as it can propagate electronic word of mouth which can be critical to remove the uncertainty in readers mind.(Hajli et. al, 2014) However, there is a possibility of similar negative kind of propagation in the social media content either by rival company or some loyalist of the company, that has nothing to do with the product features. There is evidence that just as customer behavior reinforces the market, the market also can reinforce the customer behavior(Wood & Burchell, 2018). How this kind of negative - electronic word of mouth impacts the mind of the reader while reviewing customer reviews. Such study needs to address also the possibility of similar negative reviews that have nothing to do with product features or performance.

Analysis of reviews of service related to product

Most of the studies have either touched upon the service sector as in hotel services ((Lee et. al (2022) and Ban & Kim ,2018) or product sector as in cosmetic products(Haddara et. al, 2020) and amazon product(–) as focus of customer satisfaction and opinion mining. However, to understand the customer satisfaction through customer relation, we need to further study on products and services that are interrelated, and how customer satisfaction and customer reviews of these services and products correlates to each other. There needs to be research in the area of customer and service support transcripts to better understand the credibility of the customer reviews and direct the product and service based decision with more accuracy.

Real life application

Customer satisfaction and customer review analysis can be seen as a bilateral process to improve the product as well as the business processes developing the products(Wood & Burchell, 2018). Customer satisfaction and customer review analysis can be an automated process through which an organization can improve customer relations and listen to the preferences and peeves of customers to design better products and services. Sentiments found in the trends of social media are not enough to understand a customer's needs(Lipizzi et. al, 2015) and in the new era, where there is a realm of products which needs to be customized as per customer segments needs further development in the area of customer preference and satisfaction in addition to the trends in social media.

Implications

Practical Implications

This study shows how different aspects of customer reviews have been used to analyze the product or service as well as the perspective of the end user of the product or service. Evolution in the business process previously has been based on the empirical evidence of how a product/service should be best designed. Development of new products and services is a very important process for an organization in this competitive age(Golder & Mitra, 2018). However, disposal of huge data around the perception of end users about products and services provided by an organization or company is the most vital aspect when designing business processes that are suited to deliver the best products objectively and not subjectively. Hence, development of new product as well as service

In today's market, competition for a single product needs a customer first perspective at the implementation phase of product design. Customer perspective can be best understood by different techniques shown in this study. The practical application of effective product analysis can improve the already known business process and product design process as well as infer an higher level of product delivery framework that can encourage involvement of customers more.

Theoretical Implications

In theory, the product analysis based on customer reviews can also help understand the trends of emotions running through different customer segments and it can provide us an additional perspective to understand the semantics of language in regards to customers segmentation based on their loyalty to organization or to any entity.

Conclusion and Future Research

This study involved review of literature of the past decade on customer reviews and product analysis based on customer satisfaction. The literature used various techniques of sentiment analysis, latent semantic analysis as well as machine learning algorithm to find meaning in the textual reviews and social media content about certain products in order to provide better customer satisfaction and product design process. Current research has been based on various psychological models for customer satisfaction like the Kano model and emotional hypothesis on interpretation of customer reviews. The research has provided understanding of impact of customer review analysis on customer satisfaction and product design processes.

In this literature review, There is limitation as it has not involved business processes that are attached to product design and its analysis. To execute both product design processes and product analysis in coherence the product design processes are further needed to study within the iota of product analysis. As discussed above there is scope for research in accordance with comparison of product and service review analysis and it requires thorough analysis to understand the impact of intangible nature of the services on customer reviews and electronic word of mouth(eWOM). To understand the impact of customer reviews on product sales there needs to be further research where customer reviews on two different versions of product and similar product from two different companies are analyzed and mined for customer satisfaction evaluation. This literature review is limited in the sense of analysis of literature available on customer review analysis in accordance with product analysis as it only reviews literature from the past decade.
